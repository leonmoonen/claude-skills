# FRIPRO Researcher Project for Experienced Scientists — Call Facts

Source: Research Council of Norway (RCN), "Researcher Project for Experienced Scientists (FRIPRO)" call
(https://www.forskningsradet.no/en/call-for-proposals/2025/researcher-project-experienced-scientists-fripro/),
FRIPRO general information page (https://www.forskningsradet.no/en/Portfolios/ground-breaking/fripro/),
and budget guidance (https://www.forskningsradet.no/en/financing/how/contains/).
Facts current as of the pages last updated Dec 2025 / Feb 2026. If a review is happening close to a new
call cycle, flag to the user that they should double check the live call page for any changes (deadlines
are open-ended but rules, rates and page limits are occasionally revised).

## Who this call is for

- Project manager (PM) must have **at least 6 years since the public defence of their PhD**, or since
  obtaining associate professor qualifications (equivalent: Researcher 1/2 or Senior Researcher in the
  institute sector counts as associate-professor-level).
- If the PM has fewer than 6 years' experience, they should apply to *Researcher Project for Early Career
  Scientists* instead — RCN explicitly recommends applying to whichever call has the *lowest* eligibility
  bar the applicant qualifies for, because the competition is calibrated to that career stage and gives a
  better chance of success.
- Only approved Norwegian research organisations may be the Project Owner (applicant institution),
  possibly with other research organisations as partners. Companies cannot be partners, only subcontractors.
- The PM cannot be under a waiting period (1 year after any previous ground-breaking-research submission)
  or a submission restriction period (1–2 years, triggered by an application that scored below threshold).
- The project manager and the project administrator (the institutional signatory) must be different people.

## Project shape

- **Funding**: NOK 4,000,000–12,000,000 total.
- **Duration**: 36–96 months (3–8 years).
- **Start date**: must be 8–18 months after submission (average processing time is ~5 months, range 2–10).
- No requirement for own co-financing.
- No requirement to address societal impact — reviewers only assess this criterion sub-point if the
  applicant chooses to describe it.

## Mandatory attachments

- **Project description**: max **11 pages**, using RCN's required template. This is the core document to
  review.
- **CV of the project manager**: max **4 pages**, using RCN's CV template.
- Attachments must be in English, PDF format, and use the official templates — reviewers are told that
  content outside the official attachment types (extra websites, unsolicited attachments) is *not*
  considered in the assessment, so nothing important should be pushed into an appendix that won't be read.

## Optional attachments

- CVs (max 4 pages each) for other key project participants, if their qualifications matter to the
  assessment. Do not attach CVs for not-yet-hired PhD/postdoc candidates — this is against the rules.
- Suggested reviewers (up to 3, using RCN's template): a short competence description or named candidates
  based outside Norway with strong research experience. Optional, not binding on RCN, but worth doing if
  the field is narrow or easily misunderstood by generalists.

## Budget essentials

Cost plan has four categories — check the draft budget makes sense against these:

- **Payroll and indirect costs** — researcher time (including the PM's own time and any PhD/postdoc
  positions), calculated per sector-specific rules/rates. PhD funding capped at 3 FTE-years; postdoc funding
  is 3–4 years.
- **Procurement of R&D services** — subcontracted R&D tasks from a registered R&D-supplier partner; use is
  limited, so check the call permits it for the specific cost being proposed.
- **Equipment** — depreciation/operating costs for infrastructure, apportioned to the project's actual
  share of use and useful life. Purchases under NOK 100,000 go under operating expenses instead.
- **Other operating expenses** — travel, fieldwork, dissemination/events, open-access publishing costs,
  data-management activities, consumables. Subcontractor purchases over NOK 100,000 must be itemised.

A good budget narrative ties each major cost line back to a specific work package or deliverable — reviewers
are assessing (under Implementation) whether resources assigned to work packages are aligned with
objectives, so a budget that reads as generic or disconnected from the workplan is a real weakness worth
flagging.

## How applications are actually decided (why the review bar is so high)

- FRIPRO overall funds roughly **1 in 5** applications (21% in 2024).
- Applications get **four marks, 1–7 scale** (7 = best), one per assessment criterion (see
  `assessment-criteria.md`). **An application must score 6 or 7 on ALL FOUR criteria simply to be eligible
  for funding at all** — a single criterion scored 5 or below disqualifies the whole application, no matter
  how strong the rest is. This is the single most important fact shaping how to review a draft: there is no
  "average out a weak section" — every section has to clear the bar independently.
- Among applications that clear the qualification threshold, RCN weighs **the two Excellence criteria
  most heavily** when deciding who actually gets funded (competition for the money, on top of the
  qualification hurdle).
- At least 2 of the (minimum 3) peer reviewers must have "generalist" or "specialist" competence in the
  proposal's subject — reviewers self-rate as Specialist / Generalist / Minor based on the title, summary
  and objectives. **RCN explicitly instructs applicants to write so a generalist in the broader field can
  follow it**, not just narrow subfield specialists. A proposal that reads as impenetrable outside a narrow
  niche is a legitimate and common failure mode.
- Reviewers discuss the application together in a panel and agree on a **consensus** mark — this rewards
  proposals that pre-empt the obvious objections a mixed panel would raise, since one skeptical reviewer
  can pull the consensus mark down.
- Priority given to the underrepresented gender of the PM only as a tiebreaker among otherwise equally
  assessed applications — never a substitute for scoring at threshold.

## Ethics and other required framings

The project description template has a dedicated ethics section. Its purpose is to reassure reviewers that
the applicant has a credible plan for the project's main ethical dilemmas (data, human subjects, dual use,
animal research, etc.), not to seek ethics approval itself. If ethical issues are non-trivial for the
project, do not let this section be an afterthought — reviewers rate "appropriate consideration of ethical
issues and gender dimension" as part of the Excellence – quality of R&D activities criterion.
